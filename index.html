Professional Statement

I'm a game developer and musician driven by curiosity, quiet determination, and a love of complex, meaningful systems. With a background in both software and sound, I bring a thoughtful approach to everything I build — whether it's a narrative-driven game, a haunting score, or a small mechanic that just feels right.

I don’t rush things. I enjoy solving hard problems, exploring edge cases, and pushing past the surface level to find what really works. My path hasn’t always been easy, but it’s given me a quiet resilience that shows in my work. I value clarity, craft, and projects that leave room for reflection.

When I’m not coding or composing, I’m usually listening, observing, or walking — giving ideas the space they need to grow. I’m not the loudest person in the room, but I often pick up on what others miss.

If you're looking for someone who thrives in focused, creative environments and brings both technical depth and emotional intuition to the table, I’m always open to meaningful collaborations.

About Me

Hey, I’m Sam — a game developer and composer based in Australia. I’m drawn to systems that make you think, worlds that feel slightly off-kilter, and music that lingers long after it ends.

My work is often introspective, rooted in a curiosity about the human condition, ethics, and emotional expression. Whether I’m building complex mechanics in Unity, scoring music to reflect a psychological state, or shaping narrative through interactivity, I aim to create experiences that resonate.

I value minimalism, clarity, and intentional design. The best work often happens quietly — through disciplined iteration, reflection, and a focus on what truly matters. I’m not here to chase noise; I care about impact, integrity, and letting the work speak for itself.

Philosophy

I create because I enjoy it. There’s a calm in building something from the ground up — whether it’s a piece of music, a system, or an experience that leaves an impression.

I’m not chasing trends or trying to impress anyone. I just like making things that work, feel good to use, and carry a piece of who I am. If someone connects with it, that’s a bonus — but for me, it’s about the process.

Creativity isn’t about noise or validation. It’s about tuning into what feels real and following that instinct until it becomes something worth sharing.
